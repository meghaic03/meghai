Hi Sunwoo,

After our conversation I kept thinking about the bug you mentioned that your team encountered, where legal aid staff weren't checking Haven's dashboard at all, and instead living in the LegalServer CRM. It stuck with me because it was an interesting problem — how can you perfect a dashboard people aren't even using?

You had mentioned that legal aid staff are non-technical, resource-constrained, and already overwhelmed. Asking them to adopt a new platform on top of their existing CRM sounds like a behavior change problem, not a problem that can get solved by creating better UI.

I tried building something — instead of asking staff to come to Haven, what if Haven came to them in a format they are comfortable engaging with, like in their inbox, where they already are?

Here's what I designed:

01 · Urgent alert

High-risk cases, delivered instantly

When Haven flags a high-risk case, like domestic violence, stalking, or an imminent court date, staff get an email immediately, with no login required. The urgency reason, caller info, callback window, and a one-tap link to the full transcript are all highlighted. In these urgent cases, there is an option for the staff to enable text messages specific to the case.

03 · An important insight

Overwhelming notifications become background noise

After building the first version, I shared the concept with a friend who works as a paralegal at an immigration law firm. She pointed out that if Haven sends an email for every case, the inbox risks becoming easy to ignore.

That resonated with me — at a marketing job I had, every new lead triggered an email notification. Because every lead triggered one, the inbox became noise, and the habit became auto-delete. Signals disappear with volume.

So, I added one more piece: an inbox setup guide sent during onboarding, which allows staff to set up two filters channeling urgent emails and daily digests into two dedicated folders.

Keep Haven alerts organized in Gmail

Step 1 — Filter for urgent alerts

1

Open Gmail and click the search bar. Click the filter icon on the right.

2

From: alerts@safehavenai.org · Subject: URGENT. Click Create filter.

3

Check Apply the label → New label → name it Haven — Urgent. Also check Always mark as important. Click Create filter.

Step 2 — Filter for daily digests

1

Repeat the same steps, but set Subject to Your Haven digest.

2

Name the label Haven — Digest. No need to mark as important. Click Create filter.

✓

Urgent alerts land in Haven — Urgent and are marked as important. Digests go to Haven — Digest. Your main inbox stays clean.
